Geography and Climate in Bangladesh

Bangladesh has an area of around 147,540km, making it a very densely populated country. It is completely surrounded by India and Myanmar. It is a flat country that barely rises above the sea level with fertile soil.There is a very big and complex river system in Bangladesh too. The flat land and big and complex river system along with the rain makes Bangladesh very prone to flooding.

Bangladesh has high humidity, high temperatures, and a lot of rain in the summer months, a typical monsoon climate. There are three recognized seasons, a hot and humid summer from March to June, a humid and rainy monsoon season from June to November, and a warm and dry winter from December to February. The temperature range is between 20 and 41 degrees Celsius. Most of the country receives at least 2,300mm of rainfall per year.
